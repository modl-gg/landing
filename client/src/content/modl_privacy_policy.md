# Privacy Policy

**Last Updated: May 21, 2026**

## 1. Introduction

modl.gg is a moderation and support platform for Minecraft server communities, operated by byteful studios LLC, a limited liability company registered in the Commonwealth of Kentucky, United States ("byteful studios," "we," "our," or "us"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, share, and protect information when you use modl.gg (the "Service").

Please read this Policy together with our Terms of Service and our Data Processing Addendum, which together with this Policy form a single agreement with you.

## 2. Our Two Roles: Controller and Processor

The Service involves two different kinds of personal data, and our responsibilities differ for each:

- **Account data (we are the controller).** For information about server owners and staff who hold accounts with us, such as account email addresses and billing details, we decide how and why the data is used, so we act as a data controller.
- **Player data (we are the processor).** For information about Minecraft players that a server collects and stores through the Service, such as player UUIDs, IP addresses, chat logs, and moderation records, the server owner decides how and why the data is used. The server owner is the data controller, and we act as a data processor that handles the data on the server owner's behalf and under their instructions.

Our processing of player data is governed by our Data Processing Addendum. If you are a player and you have a question or request about your data, please contact the server you played on, because that server owner controls your data. We will support server owners in responding to those requests.

## 3. Information We Collect

### 3.1 Account Information

When a server owner registers, we collect the administrator email address, the server name, and the chosen panel subdomain or custom domain. We also collect information about staff members added to a server panel, such as their names, roles, and linked Minecraft accounts.

### 3.2 Billing Information

When you purchase a paid plan, payments are processed by our payment processor, Stripe. We do not store full payment card numbers. We retain billing records such as your Stripe customer and subscription identifiers, your plan, billing status, billing period dates, and metered usage such as storage and AI usage.

### 3.3 Support Content

We collect the content of communications you send to us, such as support requests and enterprise inquiries.

### 3.4 Information Collected Automatically

When you use the Service, we automatically collect:

- **Usage data:** how you interact with the panel and the Service.
- **Log data:** access times, browser type, pages requested, and the IP address of the device used to access the Service.
- **Device information:** general information about the device and browser used.

### 3.5 Player Data We Process on Behalf of Servers

When a server uses the Service, we process the following categories of player data on the server owner's behalf:

- **Player identifiers:** Minecraft UUIDs and usernames.
- **Player IP addresses:** IP addresses recorded when players connect, along with login timestamps. IP addresses are used to detect linked or alternate accounts, which is a core moderation feature.
- **Approximate location and network data:** country, region, network operator (ASN), and proxy or hosting detection, derived from player IP addresses using a third-party geolocation service.
- **Session data:** playtime and session details.
- **Chat logs and chat snapshots:** chat messages, and full context chat snapshots captured when a player is chat-reported. Chat is recorded regardless of whether AI moderation is enabled.
- **Moderation records:** punishments, punishment history, notes, reports, appeals, and related staff actions.
- **Tickets and evidence:** support tickets, appeals, and files or links submitted as evidence.
- **Replays:** where a server uses the replay feature, gameplay recordings used to review reported incidents, along with related upload metadata.

## 4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use a small number of cookies and similar technologies:

- **Authentication cookies:** strictly necessary cookies that keep you signed in to the panel. These are set as HttpOnly and Secure.
- **Ticket attribution cookies:** cookies that confirm follow-up replies in a support ticket come from the same person who opened it.
- **Security challenge:** registration and certain forms use Cloudflare Turnstile to prevent automated abuse, which may set its own cookies or local storage.

We do not use advertising cookies, and we do not use third-party analytics or tracking cookies on the Service. Strictly necessary cookies cannot be disabled without breaking core functionality.

## 5. How We Use Information and Our Legal Bases

We use account and billing data to:

- Provide, operate, and maintain the Service (legal basis: performance of a contract).
- Process payments and manage subscriptions (legal basis: performance of a contract).
- Communicate with you about your account, security, and changes to the Service (legal bases: performance of a contract and legitimate interests).
- Secure the Service, prevent abuse, and enforce our Terms (legal basis: legitimate interests).
- Improve the Service and develop new features (legal basis: legitimate interests).
- Comply with legal obligations (legal basis: legal obligation).

We process player data only to provide the Service to the relevant server owner, on that server owner's instructions, as described in our Data Processing Addendum. The legal basis for processing player data is determined by the server owner as controller.

## 6. AI-Assisted Moderation

If a server enables AI-assisted moderation, then when a chat message is reported, the relevant chat content from that report is sent to our AI provider, Google, through the Gemini API, to analyze the report and assist staff. AI analysis runs only on chat-reported content. It does not scan all chat. We do not use player content to train our own models. Google processes the content as our subprocessor in order to return a result.

## 7. How We Share Information and Our Subprocessors

We do not sell personal information, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising.

We share information with service providers ("subprocessors") that help us operate the Service. Each is bound by contract to protect the data and to use it only to provide their service to us:

- **Stripe:** payment processing and subscription billing.
- **Google (Gemini API):** AI-assisted moderation analysis of chat-reported content.
- **Cloudflare:** bot protection through Turnstile, and custom domain routing.
- **ip-api.com:** IP address geolocation enrichment for player records.
- **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure:** hosting of the Service and its database, and delivery of account, verification, and notification emails.
- **Backblaze B2:** object storage for uploaded files, evidence, and replays.

We may also disclose information:

- **For legal reasons:** when required by law, regulation, legal process, or a governmental request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of byteful studios, our users, or the public.
- **In a business transfer:** in connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, subject to this Policy.

A current list of subprocessors is maintained as part of our Data Processing Addendum, and we will update it as our subprocessors change.

## 8. International Data Transfers

We are based in the United States, the Service is hosted in the United States, and our subprocessors may process data in the United States and other countries. If you or your players are located outside the United States, information will be transferred to and processed in the United States and other jurisdictions that may have different data protection laws. Where required, we rely on appropriate safeguards, such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, for these transfers.

## 9. Data Retention

We retain account and billing data for as long as your account is active, and afterward only as long as needed to meet legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.

For player data, retention is controlled by the server owner:

- **Replays** are retained for the period configured by the server owner, between 1 and 365 days, and are then automatically deleted.
- **Chat logs, moderation records, and other player data** are retained while the server account is active, until deleted by the server owner, or until the account is closed.
- When a server account is deleted, the associated server database and stored files are deleted, subject to short-term backups and to any retention required by law.

Some short-lived records, such as pending uploads and verification codes, expire automatically.

## 10. Data Security

We take reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect personal data, including:

- Encryption of data in transit using TLS.
- Encryption of stored data at rest where provided by our infrastructure and object storage providers.
- Authentication, role-based access controls, and least-privilege access.
- Logical separation of each server's data into its own database.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

## 11. Data Breach Notification

If we become aware of a personal data breach affecting your information, we will notify affected account holders, and, where we act as a processor, the relevant server owner, without undue delay. We will cooperate as needed so that controllers can meet their own notification obligations under applicable law.

- Receive a copy of your data in a portable format.

## 14. Children's Privacy

The Service is a business tool intended for Minecraft server operators and their staff. You must be at least 13 years old to hold an account, and the Service is not directed to children. We do not target children, and we do not knowingly create accounts for children.

Minecraft communities often include players who are children. The Service does not ask players for their age and has no way to identify which players are children, so it cannot single out or separately handle children's data. Where the Service processes player data, the server owner is the controller of that data and is responsible for complying with laws that protect children, including the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) in the United States, and the rules on children's data under the GDPR and the UK GDPR. This includes obtaining any required parental consent and providing any required notices to players and their parents. Our role and obligations as a processor of this data are set out in the Data Processing Addendum.

We do not knowingly use player data for any purpose other than providing the Service to the server owner. If we learn that we have inadvertently collected personal data directly from a child in a way not covered by a server owner's instructions, we will delete it.
