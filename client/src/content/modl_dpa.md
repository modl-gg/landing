# Data Processing Addendum

**Last Updated: May 21, 2026**

## 1. Overview

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") is part of the Terms of Service between you ("Customer," "you") and byteful studios LLC ("byteful studios," "we," "us"). It applies whenever you use modl.gg (the "Service") to process personal data about the players on your Minecraft server ("Player Data").

In plain terms: the Service lets you collect data about your players. You decide what to collect and why, so you are the controller. We handle that data for you, so we are the processor. This DPA sets out the rules for that arrangement.

## 2. Roles of the Parties

- **Customer is the controller.** You determine the purposes and means of processing Player Data, and you are responsible for the lawfulness of that processing.
- **byteful studios is the processor.** We process Player Data only on your behalf and on your documented instructions, as set out in this DPA, the Terms, and the configuration choices you make in the Service.
- For personal data about you and your staff as account holders, byteful studios is an independent controller, and that processing is governed by our Privacy Policy rather than this DPA.

## 3. Customer Instructions

Your instructions to us are: process Player Data as needed to provide the Service described in the Terms, including the moderation, support, reporting, analytics, AI-assisted moderation where you enable it, and replay features that you choose to use, and as further instructed through the Service settings. We will not process Player Data for any other purpose. If we believe an instruction violates applicable data protection law, we will inform you.

## 4. Customer Responsibilities

You represent that:

- You have a valid legal basis to collect and process Player Data through the Service.
- You have provided your players with all required privacy notices, including notice that IP addresses, chat logs, and moderation records are collected, and that reported chat may be processed by an AI provider if you enable AI-assisted moderation.
- You comply with laws protecting children, including obtaining verifiable parental consent where required by COPPA, the GDPR, the UK GDPR, or similar laws, before children's Player Data is processed through the Service.
- You will respond to your players' data rights requests, with our support as described in Section 7.

## 5. Nature of the Processing

- **Subject matter:** provision of the modl.gg moderation and support Service.
- **Duration:** the term of the Terms, plus the deletion period described in Section 9.
- **Purpose:** moderation, support, reporting, analytics, and related features selected by the Customer.
- **Types of Player Data:** Minecraft UUIDs and usernames; player IP addresses and login timestamps; approximate location and network data; session and playtime data; chat logs and chat snapshots; moderation records, notes, reports, and appeals; tickets and uploaded evidence; replays and replay metadata.
- **Categories of data subjects:** players who interact with the Customer's Minecraft server.

Player Data may relate to children, because Minecraft communities often include children. The Customer is responsible for the matters described in Section 4.

## 6. Security

We will implement and maintain appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect Player Data, including:

- Encryption of data in transit using TLS.
- Encryption of stored data at rest where provided by our infrastructure and object storage providers.
- Authentication, role-based access controls, and least-privilege access.
- Logical separation of each server's data into its own database.
- Automatic deletion of replays after the retention period that you configure.

We ensure that personnel authorized to process Player Data are bound by confidentiality obligations.

## 7. Data Subject Requests

If a player asks to access, correct, delete, or export their data, that request should be directed to you as the controller. The Service provides tools that let you locate, edit, export, and delete Player Data. If a player contacts us directly, we will refer them to you, and we will provide reasonable assistance to help you respond within the time required by law.

## 8. Subprocessors

You authorize us to engage subprocessors to help provide the Service. Each subprocessor is bound by data protection obligations no less protective than this DPA. Our current subprocessors are:

- **Stripe** (United States): payment and subscription processing.
- **Google, Gemini API** (United States): AI-assisted moderation analysis of reported chat.
- **Cloudflare** (global): bot protection and custom domain routing.
- **ip-api.com**: IP geolocation enrichment.
- **Oracle Cloud Infrastructure** (United States): hosting of the Service and its database, and delivery of account and notification emails.
- **Backblaze B2** (United States): object storage for files, evidence, and replays.

We will give you advance notice of any new or replacement subprocessor by email or through the Service. If you reasonably object to a new subprocessor on data protection grounds, you may notify us within 30 days, and we will work in good faith to address the concern. If we cannot, you may terminate the affected part of the Service.

## 9. Return and Deletion of Data

On expiry or termination of the Terms, we will delete Player Data associated with your account within 90 days, except for: (a) data in routine backups, which is deleted on the normal backup rotation; and (b) data we must retain to comply with law. Before deletion, you may export Your Content as described in the Terms. Replays are deleted automatically according to the retention period that you configure.

## 10. Personal Data Breach

If we become aware of a personal data breach affecting Player Data, we will notify you without undue delay, and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware where feasible. The notice will include the information reasonably available to us to help you meet your own notification obligations. We will take reasonable steps to mitigate the breach.

## 11. Audits

On reasonable written request, and no more than once per year unless required by a supervisory authority, we will make available the information reasonably necessary to demonstrate our compliance with this DPA. Audits must respect the confidentiality and security of other customers' data.

## 12. International Transfers

We are based in the United States, and Player Data may be processed in the United States and other countries. Where Player Data is transferred from the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the parties agree that the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses, and the UK Addendum where applicable, are incorporated into this DPA by reference and apply to that transfer.

## 13. Liability and Precedence

The liability limits in the Terms apply to this DPA. If there is a conflict between this DPA and the rest of the Terms regarding the processing of Player Data, this DPA controls.
